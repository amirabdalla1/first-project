# first-project# 

☕ Coffee Sales Data Analysis Project

Just completed my first end-to-end Data Analysis project — and the lessons started before I even built a single visual!

I thought I had discovered a major sales spike... until I fixed the date sorting. 😅  
That moment reminded me how critical clean, well-structured data is before drawing any conclusions.

---

## 📊 Project Overview
I built an interactive Power BI dashboard using a real coffee shop sales dataset to master the fundamentals of data analytics — from raw data to actionable business insights.

---

## 🔍 What I Did:
- Cleaned and transformed raw data using Excel & Power Query  
- Built a relational data model across multiple tables  
- Created DAX measures for key KPIs: Revenue, Orders, Quantity, and Hourly trends  

---

## 🛠️ Tools Used:
- Microsoft Excel  
- Microsoft Power BI  

---

## 📈 What the Dashboard Shows:
- Monthly, daily & hourly revenue trends  
- Top-performing product categories driving sales  
- Customer preferences by size & category  
- Revenue breakdown by store location  

---

## 💡 Key Insights:
- ⏰ Peak hours: **08:00–10:00 AM** — generating **$170K** in sales  
- 📅 Monday is the highest-performing day with **$101K** in revenue  
- 🗓️ June recorded the highest monthly revenue at **$166K**  
- ☕ Coffee is the top-contributing category with **$269K** in sales  

---

## 🚀 Suggestions & Recommendations:

### ⏰ Peak Hours (8:00 – 10:00 AM)
- **Reverse Happy Hour 🎉**  
  Introduce discounts and promotions after 10:00 AM to drive traffic during slower hours and maintain steady revenue throughout the day.

### 📅 Day of the Week
- **Weekly Loyalty Program 🎁**  
  Reward customers who visit more than once per week with points or discounts to encourage repeat visits on slower days.

### ☕ Top Products (Coffee, Tea & Bakery)
- **Product of the Day 🌟**  
  Highlight a featured product with a special price on low-traffic days to attract more customers.
